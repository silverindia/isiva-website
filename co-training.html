Course Outcome Design & Review

Learn to write, evaluate, and map measurable Course Outcomes aligned to Bloom's taxonomy — then work on your own COs live using the interactive tools below.

📐
1 · What is a Course Outcome?
Definition · Bloom's pyramid · NBA guidelines
▾

A Course Outcome (CO) is a specific, observable, and measurable statement of what a student will be able to do at the end of a course. Written in terms of student performance, not teacher activity.

The test: Can you assess whether a student has achieved this? If yes — it is a good CO.

Every CO must have three parts

ElementMeaningExample
Action verbSingle Bloom's verb — observable and measurableDesign
Knowledge / skillSpecific content the student demonstratesa heat exchanger for given thermal specifications
Context / standardConditions or performance standardusing IS standards and sustainability considerations
Complete CO: "Design a heat exchanger for given thermal specifications using IS standards and sustainability considerations." [Level 5 → PO1, PO3, PO7]

Common mistakes

MistakeWrongCorrect
Vague verbUnderstand thermodynamicsExplain the laws of thermodynamics and their engineering applications
Multi-verbAnalyse and design and evaluate circuitsSplit into three separate COs
Topic statementUnit 3: Fluid MechanicsApply Bernoulli's equation to solve pipe flow problems
UnmeasurableAppreciate the importance of ethicsApply ethical frameworks to resolve professional dilemmas
Too many COs15 COs for a 3-credit course5–8 COs; one per 5–8 contact hours

Bloom's Revised Taxonomy

Six cognitive levels from basic recall at the base to complex creation at the apex. Every CO must be anchored to one level using an action verb from that tier.

Create / Evaluate — higher-order
Analyse / Apply — NBA core for UG
Understand / Remember — foundational

NBA Criterion 2.2: Every course must have 5–8 COs. Each CO must map to at least one of the 12 NBA Programme Outcomes (POs) with a strength of 1, 2, or 3.

About

Twenty years across two continents

Dr. Siva is currently Professor of Mechanical Engineering, having most recently served at Mohan Babu University, Tirupati (2024–2026) following over 17 years at Kalasalingam University, where he rose from Lecturer to Professor and led the Centre for Composite Materials.

A postdoctoral fellowship at the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS), Brazil — funded by CNPq-Brazil — anchored a research direction in hybrid natural-fiber composites that continues today, alongside an international collaborator network spanning Brazil, Malaysia, Finland, Portugal, Turkey, China and South Africa.

Research focus

What the work is about

Four threads run through three decades of publications — material, behavior, application.

Materials

Natural fiber composites

Coconut sheath, curauá, basalt, luffa, sisal and water hyacinth fibers, chemically treated and woven into polyester and epoxy matrices.

Behavior

Tribology & vibration

Wear, friction, free-vibration and damping response of hybrid and sandwich composite laminates under varying load and environment.

Structures

Fiber metal laminates

Interfacial adhesion, drilling, indentation creep and impact response of GLARE-type and BLARE-type aluminum-composite laminates.

Sustainability

Waste-to-product systems

Converting aquatic waste plants, PET bottles and agricultural residues into reinforced composite products — circular-economy materials science.
